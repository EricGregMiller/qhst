Biography of R.P. Nettelhorst

       The editor of Quartz Hill Journal of Theology and Academic Vice President of Quartz Hill School of Theology. He also serves as the School's Professor of Bible and Biblical Languages.
       He is a graduate of Los Angeles Baptist College with a B.A. (Summa Cum Laude) in history and a minor in Bible; he has a M.A. in Semitic Languages from UCLA (in the Department of Near Eastern Languages and Cultures) where he has completed the course work for a Ph.D. in the same subject.
       He has published articles in the Journal of the Evangelical Theological Society and Biblical Research Monthly. He is listed in Who's Who in Religion and Who's Who in America and Who's Who in the World.
       He has several published science fiction novels and short stories to his credit. A nonfiction book, The Bible's Most Fascinating People was just published by Reader's Digest Books. It is available wherever books are sold. He is married to Ruth Nettelhorst (a third grade teacher at Nancy Cory Elementary School). They have three children, all daughters. An assortment of tropical fish, a cat, and a Golden Retriever allow them to share their house in Southern California.
