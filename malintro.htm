1. The purpose of this study is to familiarize the student with the message and theology of the book of Malachi (in the Hebrew text).

2. To accomplish this purpose the student will be required to read the book of Malachi and the comments of the professor as well as one commentary on the book. At the end of the course the student will be asked to write a 5 page paper on the meaning and theology of this book.

3. Texts: The student will need to read Malachi in his or her own Bible as well as the Hebrew text found in the course itself. The student will also need to read a commentary on Malachi of his or her choosing. Some examples (in no particular order):

H.G. Mitchell, J.H.P. Smith and J.A. Bewer. Haggai-Zechariah-Jonah-Malachi. International Critical Commentary, Old Testament. T&T Clark.

Pieter A. Verhoef. Haggai & Malachi. New International Commentary on the Old Testament. Eerdmans.

David L Petersen. Zechariah 9-14 & Malachi. The Old Testament Library. Westminster.

J. Baldwin. Haggai, Zechariah, Malachi. Tyndale Old Testament Commentary. Intervarsity.

Ralph Smith. Micah-Malachi. Word Biblical Commentary. Word.

These come from a variety of theological backgrounds.

Finally, the student will need to acquire Gosta Ahlstrom. The History of Ancient Palestine. Augsburg.

Books may be purchased through The Campus Bookstore.

4. Prerequisite: The student undertaking this course must have completed both Elementary and Advanced Hebrew.
