What Is This?

       In case you are wondering about this odd script, you might be interested to know that it is the first two words of the Babylonian creation epic. The epic is called Enuma elish, which translates as "When on high".

Portions of Enuma elish were first made available in modern times by George Smith in 1876. So far, there is no general agreement as to the date that Enuma elish was first written. The extant texts all date from after 1000 BC. However, internal evidence suggests to most scholars a date of composition during the Old Babylonian period -- that is, the early part of the second millennium BC.
       Below are the opening few lines:

When on high the heaven had not been named,
And firm ground below had not been named,
Then only ancient Apsu, their father,
And Tiamat, their mother, existed.
Their waters were mixed, forming a single body.
No reed hut had been built,
No wetlands had appeared.
When no gods whatever had been made:
Without names and their destinies undetermined --
Then it was that the gods were formed within Apsu and Tiamat.

       Note: Apsu is the god of fresh water and Tiamat is the goddess of salt water, that is, the sea.
