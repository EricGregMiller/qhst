Bridge to Nowhere Hike Information

There is a bridge that goes nowhere in the middle of nowhere in the Angeles National Forest north of Claremont. The bridge and the hike to it are reported as very interesting. Here is more information about the bridge to nowhere on Local Hikes.

Not only is the bridge to nowhere north of Claremont, it is just south of Vincent Gap which is where we started our Baden-Powell hike. This would be better for us since the drive is shorter -- about an hour and twenty minutes. On the map below a possible route is laid out. Unfortunately the hike would be 8.6 miles one way with a 2000ft. descent going in and a corresponding climb on the way out. Furthermore, the first several miles appear to have no trail. We would have to hike along (through?) whatever river runs through Vincent Gulch. This might be more than we can handle.
